catalyze your climate-centric career

through project arrowroot's program for youth

PROJECT ARROWROOT

empowering young professionals to deploy green solutions + lead impact

design your future. commit to climate. be a changemaker.

knowledge-sharing

  • understand Canada's climate action landscape by learning about upcoming trends + growing demands in green industries
  • explore different post-secondary pathways to professional success
  • learn what post-secondary programs lead to different careers, and how to pick the program for you

professional development

  • build your capacity to pursue and impact-driven career
  • learn how to form genuine professional relationships + what effective networking looks like
  • be mentored by professionals working in academia, nonprofits, government, private-sector companies, and the trades

post-secondary + career planning

  • receive university application tips + tricks
  • pick up strong resume + cover letter best practices
  • gain confidence in interviews
  • complete skill-building activities that help pinpoint your passion

micro-internship

  • experience firsthand what an impact-driven career means by spending a week shadowing your mentor at their company or organization
  • gain unique insights into how people are shaping our futures with their career
  • spearhead a mini research project for your assigned organization

receive mentorship from industry professionals at organizations like:

1.

register!

submit a short application after checking your eligibility.

the Youth Activator Program is designed for highschool students (grades 11-12 preferred) and any post-secondary students across Canada who:

  • are curious about climate change and want to learn more
  • care about making a positive impact but aren't sure how
  • want to explore career options after high school
  • need support with university/college applications
  • are looking to build professional skills and confidence
  • want to connect with mentors in climate-related fields
  • are passionate about social justice and environmental issues

you don't need to be a topic expert or have perfect grades. we welcome young people at all knowledge levels who are curious, committed, and ready to learn.

time commitment: 1.5 hours/week.

2.

interview!

chat with us for 20 minutes so we can get to know you better!

we’ll talk about:

  • your passions, interests, and hobbies
  • what you enjoy learning about
  • environmental + social issues you deeply care for

3.

workshops

spend ~2hrs a week understanding Canada’s climate landscape, building your problem-solving capacity, and growing your network.

topics we cover:

  • climate action in the context of government, academia, nonprofits, trades, and the corporate sector
  • how to stand out in an increasingly competitive market
  • university applications, resumes, cover letters, and networking
  • building genuine professional relationships

4.

micro internship

experience firsthand what an impact-driven career means by shadowing your mentor at their company or organization!

you’ll get to:

  • gain unique insights into how people are shaping our world with their career
  • spearhead a mini research project for your assigned organization

frequently asked questions:

how is this program delivered?

this is a virtual program, with site visit opportunities and an in-person event at the end of the program!

are there any fees associated with participating?

no, we will never charge youth to join our program!

how much experience do I need to join?

we value passion + willingness to learn more than experience!

still have questions?

send us an email at aliao@projectarrowroot.ca!
