1. register!

submit a short application after checking your eligibility

we want:
  • high school students in grade 11-12
  • 2-3 hours of your time every week from October to March
we don’t need:
  • any previous experience!
  • a defined academic/career plan

2. interview

chat with us for 15 minutes so we can get to know you better!

we’ll talk about:
  • your passions, interests, and hobbies
  • what you enjoy learning about
  • environmental + social issues you deeply care about

3. workshops

spend ~2hrs a week from October to February understanding Canada’s climate landscape, building your problem-solving capacity, and growing your network.

topics we cover:
  • climate action in the context of government, academia, nonprofits, trades, and the corporate sector
  • university applications, resumes, cover letters, and linkedin
  • building genuine professional relationships

4. micro internship

experience firsthand what an impact-driven career means by spending a week shadowing your mentor at their company or organization during spring break!

you’ll get to:
  • gain unique insights into how people are shaping our futures with their career
  • university applications, resumes, cover letters, and linkedin
  • spearhead a mini research project for your assigned organization
