support our work

empower + catalyze youth leadership

help us empower the next generation of climate leaders, social impact practitioners, and young professionals.

see past partnerships here

this is why your support matters:

the climate crisis demands urgent action—but young people are told it's their problem to inherit without being given the tools to address it. only 6 of 13 Canadian provinces include climate education in their curriculum. meanwhile, 78% of youth report climate anxiety, yet only 25% see their career as a way to take action.

Project Arrowroot bridges this gap. we provide free, accessible climate education and professional development to high school students across BC and Canda—with a focus on equity-deserving youth who face the most barriers to accessing green careers.

when you support our work, you're not just funding a program. you're investing in:

a student from a low-income family gaining tailored mentorship they couldn't otherwise access

a first-generation newcomer youth receiving support on pathways to post-secondary education that their parents may not be sure how to provide

a young person transforming climate anxiety into climate agency, and turning an idea into action

ways to support:

  • granting + -

    • every contribution—no matter the size—directly supports Youth Activators and other climate education and professional development programming. your donation helps us:

    • keep the program completely free and accessible to all students and educators
    • provide honorariums to guest speakers from diverse backgrounds
    • provide stipends for youth transportation to in-person events and activities
    • develop curriculum materials and digital resources
    • employ part-time staff to keep everything running smoothly
    • expand outreach to students in other regions, including rural and remote communities

    if interested in granting, please contact aliao@projectarrowroot.ca

  • become a mentor + host a micro internship + -

    • hare your expertise and open doors for the next generation. our mentors work in social impact and climate-related fields across all sectors—from renewable energy project managers to municipal urban planners, social enterprise founders to impact investors.

    • time commitment: ~5-10 hours over 1-2 weeks.
    • what you’ll do: host a Youth Activator for a flexible micro internship, facilitate a small research project in line with your work, and share insights about your academic and career path
  • organizational partnership + -

    • is your organization interested in supporting youth climate education? we're currently developing sponsorship packages for companies, foundations, and institutions aligned with our mission. partnership opportunities include:

    • cohort sponsorship (fund a full cohort of 10+ students)
    • event sponsorship (program-end celebration events, career panels, site/workplace visits and field trips)
    • in-kind support (workspace access, software licenses, marketing support)

    if you’re a like-missioned organization, we’re always open to working together. in the past, we have collaborated with other organizations on hosting focus groups, event planning, curriculum development, and more :)

    reach out to aliao@projectarrowroot.ca with any questions or collaboration ideas!

  • spread the word + -

    • you can also make waves by:

    educators + youth workers everyone
    • share our program with students in grades 11-12 or early post-secondary
    • display our posters in classrooms, community centres, or youth spaces
    • invite us to present to your students or organization
    • follow us on social media: Instagram, LinkedIn
    • share the Youth Activator program with young people in your life who might benefit
    • forward our program info and in-class offerings to guidance counselors, teachers, and youth leaders

transparency + accountability

we believe in meaningful transparency about how funds are used. as a by-youth, for-youth organization, we keep overhead minimal so maximum impact goes directly to the young people receiving and delivering our programs.

2025 - 2026 budget breakdown:

renumeration: 78%

digital tools: 1%

supplies & equipment: 14%

honorariums: 7%
