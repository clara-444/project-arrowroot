mentor

practice intra/intergenerational knowledge exchange

what do Youth Activator mentors do?

share your expertise and open doors for the next generation. Our mentors work in social impact and climate-related fields across all sectors—from renewable energy project managers to municipal urban planners, social enterprise founders to impact investors.

this means that every young person in our program gets an authentic look into the many ways to build an impactful career, guided by a professional who proves that a passion for the planet can translate into countless fulfilling professions.

no matter a student's passion— whether it's policy, technology, or community organizing—we can connect them with a relatable role model who has walked a similar path, empowering them to see the vast landscape and find their own specific place within the movement.

  • Paluck Kohli + -
    • paluck kohli

    Mentor: Paluck Kohli (she/her)

    Paluck was born and raised in New Delhi, India and moved to Turtle Island 6 years ago. As a newcomer she was deeply curious about the history of the land and immersed herself in learning more about indigenous people, their ways of knowing and ways of being. She currently works as a Manager, Indigenous Relations at City of Vancouver, driving outcomes measurement for United Nations Rights for Indigenous Peoples at the municipal level.

    She has a deep passion for community-rooted measurement and in her previous role, she worked as an impact measurement specialist with Social Innovation Canada on a UNSDG project in partnership with CCEDNet, Common Approach & CSI. She co-led the revamp of CSI Impact Dashboard and supported over 750 social purpose organizations to track their impact, define unique metrics and communicate their stories. Previously, she has held intersectional experience across climate action work; EDI and Indigeneity knowledge; business consulting as well as fintech industry. She finds home in the open water and has been a lifelong spiritual seeker.

  • Skye Noh + -
    • skye noh

    Mentor: Skye Noh (she/her)

    Skye is a climate justice advocate with a passion for using capital flows to make a positive impact. In the past, she has served as a Canada 150 youth delegate, a COP27 delegate, and a former Top 25 Under 25 Environmentalist in 2018.

    Skye is currently a Junior Associate, Private Investment Research at Rally Assets and also Realize Capital Partners, one of the three wholesalers of the national Social Finance Fund.

    Mentor: Mackenzie Kuenz (she/her)

    Mackenzie (she/her) grew up on Tsuut’ina and W̱SÁNEĆ territories and now lives on the traditional and stolen territories of the Xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Səl̓ilwətaɁɬ, and Sḵwx̱wú7mesh Nations.     Mackenzie is passionate about education and empowering youth to create innovative solutions and initiatives that challenge the status quo and harmful systems. She is the CityShapers in Communities Coordinator where she delivers workshops to youth community groups helping them understand how our cities and systems work and how youth can shape cities. She is also the co-founder and co-director of Ritmos Climáticos an organization mobilizing culture for climate justice through events, campaigns, and a digital community. She loves her community and dancing.
